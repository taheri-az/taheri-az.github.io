I am a PhD researcher at Northeastern University working on high-level planning and decision-making for autonomous systems in unknown environments. My research bridges formal methods and robotic autonomy to enable provably correct behavior in complex, uncertain settings.

Research Interests
======

- **Temporal logic-based task planning** — using LTL and scLTL specifications to define and enforce complex robot behaviors
- **Autonomous navigation** — enabling robots to operate in previously unseen environments with incomplete information
- **Decision-making under uncertainty** — designing planning frameworks that remain correct despite partial observability
- **Multi-agent systems** — coordination and planning for teams of heterogeneous robots (e.g., UAV-UGV teams)

Research Focus
======

My goal is to design provably correct planning frameworks that allow robots to:

- Operate in previously unseen, semantically unknown environments
- Adapt to uncertainty and incomplete information at runtime
- Satisfy complex temporal logic specifications throughout their mission

A recent focus is integrating temporal logic with frontier-based exploration so that a robot can explore an unknown environment while simultaneously satisfying high-level task specifications.
